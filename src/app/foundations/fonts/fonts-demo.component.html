Font Guidelines

Last updated Sep 12, 2025
Display Typefaces

Archivo

Our brand display typeface is Archivo.

Picked for its versatility, Archivo is a versatile, modern sans-serif font that is functional but feels warm. It was designed to be used simultaneously in print and online platforms and supports over 200 world languages.

Headlines can use Archivo Extra-bold or Archivo Light when the headline is large. Subheads should use Semi-bold or Medium subheadings, and the medium weight should be used on body copy. Headlines should be about twice the size of the subheading, and subheads should be about twice the size of the body copy.

The core font family is available in seven weights, but only the weights listed on this page are approved for use within our brand: Light, Medium, SemiBold, Bold, ExtraBold. Do not use italics.

Open Sans

Our brand copy typeface is Open Sans.

Open Sans is available in five weights, all of which are approved for use. Typically, body copy should be set in Regular, while italics and heavier weights should be applied for emphasis, quotations, links, and references.

For best legibility, a good rule of thumb is to keep columns at roughly 60 characters per line, and leading should be set somewhere between 100-150%.

Open Sans is a humanist sans serif typeface that was designed with an upright stress, open forms and a neutral, yet friendly, appearance. It was optimized for print, web, and mobile interfaces, and has excellent legibility characteristics in its letter-forms.

Secondary Typefaces

Noto Serif

A secondary body copy font that may be used in large bodies of text, but should always support headlines and other elements in the primary brand font, Archivo.

Download Noto Serif

Noto Sans

Has expanded glyph and non-Latin character sets, and should be used in web, development, and international materials. Note: the Cashmere library and this site use Noto Sans as their base font. Noto Sans is very similar to Open Sans visually, but supports all international character sets. Additional information about Google's open source Noto font family can be found at their documentation site.

Download Noto Sans

Calibri

May be used for internal documents and presentations when core primary and secondary typefaces are unavailable.
